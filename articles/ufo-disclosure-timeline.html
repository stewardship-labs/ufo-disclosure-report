January 2024 — Increased DoD Transparency Commitments

The Department of Defense reiterates its commitment to improving UAP reporting channels and releasing more unclassified summaries. This marks the beginning of a more structured disclosure period.

Multiple lawmakers publicly criticize the slow pace of UAP transparency, calling for mandatory reporting standards and independent oversight.

August 2024 — New Military Reporting Protocols

Updated UAP reporting procedures are implemented across several branches, standardizing how sightings are logged and escalated.

Previously classified Navy sensor data is partially declassified, offering new insights into anomalous flight characteristics.

A multi‑disciplinary panel of physicists, aerospace engineers, and data scientists is assembled to analyze UAP cases using standardized methodologies.

October 2025 — Congressional Hearing on UAP Data Integrity

Lawmakers question military officials about data retention, classification practices, and the need for public transparency.

2026

January 2026 — First Annual UAP Public Summary Report

A consolidated, public‑facing report is released summarizing the previous year's sightings, sensor captures, and unresolved cases.

May 2026 — International Cooperation Framework Announced

Several allied nations agree to share UAP data, marking the first coordinated global disclosure effort.

September 2026 — Civilian Scientific Access Expanded

Portions of the UAP database are opened to accredited researchers, enabling independent analysis and peer‑reviewed studies.

Summary

From 2024 to 2026, the pace of UFO/UAP disclosure accelerated dramatically. What began as sporadic releases evolved into structured reporting, international cooperation, and increased scientific involvement. This timeline will continue to expand as new events unfold.
